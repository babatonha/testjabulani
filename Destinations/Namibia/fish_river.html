Fish River Canyon

The largest canyon in Africa, as well as the second most visited tourist attraction in Namibia

Overview

It features a gigantic ravine, in total about 100 miles (160 km) long, up to 27 km wide and in places almost 550 meters deep. The Fish River is the longest interior river in Namibia. It cuts deep into the plateau which is today dry, stony and sparsely covered with hardy drought-resistant plants. The river flows intermittently, usually flooding in late summer; the rest of the year it becomes a chain of long narrow pools. At the lower end of the Fish River Canyon, the hot springs resort of Ai-Ais is situated.

Things to do

Go hiking amid the spectacular scenary of the trail or, if you prefer a faster pace & gruelling challenge, take part in the annual Fish River Canyon Ultra Marathon.
